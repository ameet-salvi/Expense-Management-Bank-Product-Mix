# Expense-Management-Bank-Product-Mix
The excel workbook helps us calculate the budget vs actuals with scenario analysis. 
7 sheets, all formula-driven, zero errors:

Read Me – structure, color legend, how to steer the model
Assumptions – Base/Upside/Downside scenario switch, every cost ratio sourced to named 2025/26 research (Gartner CMO Survey, McKinsey, AlixPartners, Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis compliance study, Glassdoor/PayScale comp data)
Product Mix – four Citi-style product lines (Credit Cards, Retail Banking, Small Business Banking, Wealth & Affluent Banking), with CAC pulled directly from First Page Sage's banking-CAC benchmark table, building up to contribution margin, LTV:CAC, and CAC-payback, each RAG-flagged
Expense Detail & Budget Control – quarterly phased budget vs. actuals with live variance and status flags
5-Yr Projection – compounding revenue, operating-leverage-adjusted expense ratios, and NPV of contribution margin, by product and total
Sensitivity & Scenarios – Year-5 outcomes under all three scenarios side by side, plus one-variable stress tests (CAC ±20%, marketing ±2pp, growth ±3pp)
Dashboard – KPI cards and four charts for a stakeholder readout

Realistic outputs it's built to surface (explained in the Read Me):

A defensible contribution-margin ranking across the mix — Credit Cards and Wealth currently look strongest; Retail Banking is right at breakeven on a stand-alone basis
An early warning that Retail Banking's $561 CAC only clears the 4x LTV:CAC target when read as a relationship/cross-sell acquisition cost, not a stand-alone deposit product — a genuine, real pattern worth flagging to stakeholders
A quarterly RAG-flagged budget cockpit for the "expense management goals" mandate
A 5-year NPV view for roadmap prioritization
Pre-built stress tests for a budget review
